SYNOPSIS

     Provides civil service preference to military servicemembers who served in humanitarian Operations Provide Comfort and Provide Comfort II.

STATEMENT

 

     This bill would provide civil service preference to military servicemembers who served in Operations Provide Comfort and Provide Comfort II, created in response to Kurds fleeing Saddam Hussein's oppressive regime.  The Provide Comfort operations were part of a humanitarian mission of the United States in conjunction with many other countries to provide Kurdish refugees with relief supplies, to enforce a security zone to protect the Kurds on the ground, and to enforce a no-fly zone over northern Iraq.  Although these operations may not be as widely recognized as more traditional missions, at the time, the mission was the largest humanitarian effort in history and it saved the lives of thousands of people.
